Personas & Storyboarding

The Stationary Bike

Prep

For my interface, I chose the stationary bike in the Bear's Lair/Grad Center E.

Key Interactive Components
  • 1. Handle buttons
  • 2. Console buttons
  • 3. Horizontal handles
  • 4. Vertical handles
  • 5. Seat height lever
  • 6. Pedals

The stationary bike attempts to recreate the experience of riding a bicycle without ever having to move from one spot. The buttons on the left handle allow the rider to pause/stop the ride, and the buttons on the right handle allow the rider to increase/decrease the time left on the ride. The buttons on the main console include the functionality of the handles and more, including modes and pausing the ride. The two sets of handles offer the rider different ways of holding the bike while pedaling. The lever beneath the seat allows the rider to adjust the seat height, and the pedals allow the rider to cycle.

Observations

Key Observations
  • The first rider I observed immediately pressed the green start button and started to pedal, while the other two riders first looked over the console.
  • All three riders first grasped the horizontal handles and not the vertical handles.
  • All three riders used the pedals correctly from the outset: each user slid his or her feet under the straps.
  • None of the three riders that I observed made use of the different ride modes (rolling hills, fat burn, etc.), but rather used the bike more simplistically.
Questions
  • 1. What is your initial reaction when looking at the control panel of the interface?
  • 2. How would you start the machine?
  • 3. How would you stop it?
  • 4. Are there any buttons that you think someone might find unclear?
  • 5. How do you feel about the beeping?
  • 6. How would you sit on the stationary bike?
  • 7. Why did you hold the handles that way?
  • 8. How would you adjust the seat?
Key Takeaways
  • 1. When asked about the handles, all three respondents described the horizontal handles as better for easy riding, while the vertical handles would be for more intense riding.
  • 2. When asked about confusing buttons, some combination of the mode buttons was mentioned by every rider, the rolling hill ride in particular. Every user did hazard guesses for some of the modes, with varying success.
  • 3. When asked how to adjust the seat, all three respondents used the yellow tongue lever underneath the seat. However, one interviewee remarked that she would not have thought to adjust the seat had she not been prompted.
  • 4. Two of the riders, when asked, responded negatively about the beeping that accompanied the countdown. One said that it was unnecessary, and the other that it drew unwanted attention toward the rider.
  • 5. When asked how to stop the bike, two riders used the big red button on the console, but one rider used the stop button on the left handle. When I pointed out that button to the other two riders, they both seemed surprised, as if that second stop button had gone unnoticed by them.

Personas

Active Arlene

Arlene is not interested in creating the perfect ride; she just wants to start riding as quickly as possible to stay warmed up and also to avoid burning out her legs. She is distracted by the ongoing game, so the few buttons she needs must be clearly demarcated.

Arlene represents the low-intensity rider that each interviewee ascribed to the horizontal handles. Moreover, she incorporates the user that immediately pressed the green button and started pedaling. Also mixed in is the other user who said she did not think about readjusting the seat.

Bike Mike

Mike wants to have a ride similar to what he experiences on his bicycle outdoors, so he is willing to take some time to explore the mode buttons that Arlene ignores, but he does not have a manual, and his patience is thin.

Mike represents the high-intensity user that was ascribed to the vertical handles. He incorporates the users whose first instincts were to look at the console and expressed some confusion at the mess of buttons, as well as the user who investigated the buttons on the handlebars. He also incorporates the two users who expressed dissatisfaction with the beeping, thus Mike doesn’t like loud noises.

Storyboard

This storyboard is for Active Arlene.

1. As Active Arlene comes off the court during a timeout, she walks straight toward the courtside stationary bike with her coach. On the way the two talk.
2. As her coach discusses changes to the game plan, Arlene gets on the bike, distractedly looks for a start button, and presses the big green button. She can't hear the beeping countdown over the noise of the crowd, so she does not remember to start at first.
3. After twenty second or so she realizes she hasn't started pedaling, so she begins. She and her coach talk a bit longer before her coach returns to the bench, and Arlene continues to pedal.
4. Arlene watches the game and keeps an eye on the scoreboard. Her attention is on the ongoing game.
5. A staffer comes over and offers Arlene a water bottle, which she accepts. She continues to pedal, but her mind is on anything but the bike.
6. After a few minutes, the coach prepares to sub Arlene back in, so she hastily hits the big red button and gets off the bike. Despite the slow start, she is still warmed up and ready to go, albeit not as limber as had she started pedaling immediately.
